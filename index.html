About Tom Erb

Professional Actor, Director, and Playwright

Tom Erb's saga in the performing arts is a rich tapestry woven with the threads of an actor, director, producer, and now, playwright. His journey began in the vibrant early 1960s when he first stepped into the spotlight, transitioning through directing in the 1970s with his debut "A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur's Court," and evolving into a comprehensive theater professional.

Years Active 1960s - Present
Specialties Actor, Director, Playwright
Location Venice, Florida
Legacy Project Newport Arts Transformation

For eight transformative years in Newport, Rhode Island, Tom worked tirelessly to turn a town focused on sailing, food, and tourism into a place with a thriving art scene. He organized staged readings, performances, radio plays, and art shows that brought much-needed creative substance to the community. Through his own grit and perseverance, he created an original Newport-based TV series, showcasing his versatility across multiple media.

Tom's contributions extended beyond the limelight as he dedicated himself to mentoring emerging talents, thereby nurturing the growth of the arts community. His educational background is as extensive as his professional experience, having worked across theater, film, radio, and television production.

Now, as an accomplished playwright, Tom is poised to distill his wealth of experience into stories he has long yearned to tell. Drawing from the well of his acting and directing experiences, he crafts plays that reflect the depth and breadth of his artistic journey. This new venture represents not just a continuation of his career but a merger of his life's work, promising to deliver narratives that resonate with authenticity and passion.

Career Timeline

Acting Career Begins
Tom's journey in the performing arts begins as he steps into the spotlight, developing his craft as an actor in the vibrant theater scene of the early 1960s. This foundational period established his deep understanding of character development and stage presence.

Transition to Directing
As new cultural tides emerged, Tom transitioned to directing, bringing meticulous attention to detail and distinctive creative vision to the stage. His directorial debut, "A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur's Court," marked the beginning of his distinguished directing career.
